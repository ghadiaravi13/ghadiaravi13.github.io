I am a GPU Power Architect at NVIDIA, Bengaluru, working on Applied Power Architecture, where I analyze and enhance GPU performance w.r.t per watt of consumed power, leveraging applied Machine Learning and Reinforcement Learning. I also work as a research assistant at H2Lab, University of Washington under Prithviraj Ammanabrolu, where I am working on deriving reward from linguistic feedback to train Language Models using RL. My research interests lie in energy-efficient designs for computer architecture, using statistical methods, Reinforcement Learning and other optimization algorithms. I am also keenly interested in NLP, particularly Language Models, RLHF, and interactive systems.

I graduated from IIT Kharagpur in 2021 with a major in Electronics and Elec. Comm. and a minor in Computer Science. My bachelor thesis was on Training Generative Dialog Models using Reinforcement Learning with Learned Reward Systems, supervised by Dr. Pawan Goyal.

My hobbies include trekking, photography, calisthenics, and a lil bit of cooking. I also love reading scientific blogs related to astronomy and physiology.
